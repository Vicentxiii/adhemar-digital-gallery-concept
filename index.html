III — The Vision

The Vision

Adhemar Cabral does not build replicas. He removes everything from an engine, a chassis, a wing — until only its essential form remains.

This is not a motorsport story. It is a study of what endures once speed is taken away.

The site we propose will not sell. It will exhibit.

  • Silence
  • Precision
  • Craftsmanship
  • Legacy
  • Luxury
  • Timelessness
  • Negative space
  • Light
  • Material
  • Architecture

Inter — minimal sans, weight 300–400. Body copy, labels, captions.

Spacing

A scale that widens deliberately. Whitespace is never left over — it is placed.

Interaction

Transitions run slow — 600 to 1200 milliseconds, eased, never bounced. Nothing on this site arrives quickly. The accent color appears only on hover, and only once per screen.

VI — User Journey

User Journey

A cinematic storyboard, not a sitemap.

  1. 01ArrivalA black screen. A name. Nothing asks for attention.
  2. 02SilenceA pause before the first work appears — the visitor slows down without noticing.
  3. 03ArtworkA single sculpture, alone, given the entire screen.
  4. 04StoryThe idea behind the piece — brief, unhurried, in the artist's own words.
  5. 05ProcessMaterial, method, hours — the craft made visible without being explained away.
  6. 06RecognitionWhere the work has stood. Institutions named plainly, without ornament.
  7. 07ContactOne line. One address. The conversation begins off-screen.
